⚠️ NEW: Annual Eligibility Redetermination now required — Effective July 1, 2026

Annual Redetermination Criteria:

• 33% delay in one developmental domain, OR

• 25% delay in two or more developmental domains

Initial Eligibility:

Children birth to 3rd birthday with 25% developmental delay in 1+ areas, OR diagnosed condition with high probability of delay.

Exceptions (No Annual Redetermination):

• Children with established (diagnosed) conditions — services continue unless family withdraws

• Children at transition age (2y 9m+) eligible for Part B or Extended Part C

Evaluation Notes:

• Evaluations during existing EI visits — not separate billable visits

• May be completed across multiple visits

• Conducted by IFSP team providers

• If only 1 provider on IFSP, a second evaluator is required

Preferred Tool:

DAYC-2 (Developmental Assessment of Young Children, 2nd Ed.)

Timelines:

• Referral → Evaluation: 45 calendar days

• Evaluation → IFSP: within 45 days of referral

• Transition planning begins at 27 months

• IFSP transition steps at 30 months

• Transition conference by 33 months

• Annual redetermination before annual IFSP meeting

Services:

SLP, OT, PT, DI, Service Coordination, Nutrition, Vision, Hearing, AT, Psychology, Social-Emotional.

Documentation:

Retain all evaluation protocols for 6 years; provide within 24 hours if requested.

Resources:

• Redetermining Eligibility Annually (Infographic) — CDEC

• FAQ — CDEC

• Annual Evaluation Process Guide — CDEC

• Annual Evaluation Billing Guide — CDEC

• Provider Office Hours — biweekly Wed 1:30-2:30 PM (starting June 10, 2026)
