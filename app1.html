Clinical Standards & Ethics

Code of Ethics

All Emmé providers are expected to uphold the ethical standards of their respective licensing board (LPC, LCSW, MFT, Psychologist, etc.) as well as those of any relevant national associations (ACA, NASW, AAMFT, APA).

Violations of ethical standards are grounds for immediate contract review and possible termination.

A copy of your licensing board's code of ethics should be reviewed annually. If you have a question about ethical decision-making, bring it to supervision immediately — not after.

Scope of Practice

Providers are expected to practice only within their scope of licensure and competence. If a client presents with needs beyond your current competencies, consult your supervisor and facilitate a referral as needed.

You are not permitted to:

  • • Provide services for which you are not trained or licensed
  • • Diagnose outside your scope
  • • Enter into dual relationships with clients
  • • Provide services while impaired

Trauma-Informed Practice

"Every intake, every session, every administrative touchpoint is an opportunity to communicate safety."

Emmé's clinical approach is rooted in the six core principles of trauma-informed care:

Documentation

Why Documentation Matters

Accurate, timely documentation protects your clients, your license, and the practice. All documentation must be completed in our EHR system. Handwritten or external notes are not accepted as the primary record.

⚠ Important: Late or missing documentation may result in a hold on billing and payroll disbursement.

Documentation Timeline

DocumentCompletion Deadline
Intake / Biopsychosocial AssessmentWithin 24 hours of first session
Diagnosis (DSM-5-TR)Within first session or as clinically appropriate
Treatment PlanWithin 3 sessions (30 days for children)
Progress Notes (SOAP or DAP format)Within 24 hours of session
Treatment Plan ReviewEvery 90 days
Crisis / Safety PlanSame day as risk assessment
Discharge SummaryWithin 5 business days of last session
Release of InformationBefore any communication with collateral contacts

Progress Note Format

Emmé uses SOAP notes (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan) as the standard format. Notes must include:

Session date, duration, modality (in-person/telehealth)
Client presentation (affect, engagement, themes discussed)
Clinical observations (objective findings)
Assessment of progress toward treatment goals
Plan for next session
Risk assessment notation (when applicable)
Homework or between-session tasks assigned
Link Placeholder — Progress Note Template

Treatment Plans

Treatment plans must include:

  • • Presenting problem and diagnostic impressions
  • • Measurable goals (minimum 2, maximum 5)
  • • Targeted interventions for each goal
  • • Estimated frequency and duration of treatment
  • • Client signature or documented verbal consent

Treatment plans must be reviewed and updated every 90 days, or sooner if there is a significant change in the client's presentation or goals.

Client Management

The Intake Process

The intake process at Emmé is designed to be warm, thorough, and efficient. Clients complete online intake paperwork before their first appointment.

Provider responsibilities at intake:

Confirm intake paperwork is complete in EHR before session
Review consents (therapy, telehealth if applicable, release of information)
Conduct biopsychosocial assessment
Complete initial risk screen
Establish initial rapport and explain your approach
Discuss fee structure (if relevant to your role)
Assign diagnosis and begin treatment plan

Scheduling Policies

All scheduling is managed through our EHR. Providers are responsible for keeping their availability updated at all times.

Policies & Compliance

HIPAA & Confidentiality

All providers must comply with HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules. You signed a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) at onboarding.

🚨 Critical: Never share client information — including name, diagnosis, or session details — via unsecured channels (personal email, text message, social media, or non-HIPAA-compliant platforms).

Permitted disclosures without client consent include:

When the client is at imminent risk of harm to self or others
When required by court order or law
For treatment, payment, or healthcare operations (within the team)
When required for mandatory reporting

All other disclosures require a signed Release of Information (ROI).

Link Placeholder — ROI Form

Mandatory Reporting (Colorado)

Colorado law requires all mental health professionals to report:

  • • Suspected child abuse or neglect
  • • Suspected elder or dependent adult abuse
  • • Imminent risk of harm to an identifiable third party (duty to warn)
When in doubt, consult your supervisor before making a report — but never delay a report if a child is in immediate danger.
Link Placeholder — Colorado Mandatory Reporting Guidelines

Crisis & Emergency Procedures

"Every provider should know the crisis protocol before they need it."

If a client is in immediate danger:

1Stay on the phone or video call with the client.
2Attempt to get their current location.
3Call 911 and request a wellness check if you cannot maintain contact.
4Notify your supervisor immediately (same day, even after hours).
5Document the crisis in EHR within 2 hours.
6Complete a Safety Plan with the client at the next available session.
